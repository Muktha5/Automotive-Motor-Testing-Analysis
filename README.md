# 🔧 Motor Durability Analysis – Internship Project

## 📌 Overview
This project focuses on evaluating the durability and performance of automotive electric motors under varying thermal and mechanical conditions.

The work was carried out during my internship, where I analyzed motor behavior across repeated cycles and different environmental conditions to assess reliability and performance trends.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze motor performance under different temperature conditions
- Study the effect of repeated cycles on durability
- Evaluate current behavior, motion characteristics, and thermal response
- Identify patterns indicating wear and performance variation

---

## 🛠 Methodology
- Motors were tested under multiple temperature environments (cold, ambient, hot)
- Load conditions were applied to simulate real-world resistance
- Performance parameters such as current, motion behavior, and response time were observed
- Comparative analysis was conducted across multiple test cycles

---

## 📊 Key Observations
- Motor performance varies significantly between upward and downward motion due to load differences
- Repeated cycles introduce minor performance changes indicating wear-in behavior
- Thermal conditions influence stability and efficiency
- Some motor variants demonstrated more consistent and stable performance than others

---

## 🔍 Insights
- Higher load conditions result in increased current demand and reduced motion speed
- Stable current patterns indicate healthy motor operation
- Variations in performance can signal early-stage wear or inefficiencies
- Thermal stability is a critical factor for long-term reliability

---

## 🚀 Conclusion
This project provided insights into how electric motors behave under real-world conditions. The analysis highlights the importance of durability testing, thermal management, and consistent performance for reliable automotive applications.
